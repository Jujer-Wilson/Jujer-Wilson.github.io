1. What is a screenshot?#

A screenshot is an image taken from a device’s display that records whatever is being shown at that particular moment. It typically captures everything displayed on the screen, including windows, icons, and any other items visible. Taking screenshots is a simple process on the iPhone 6s and is achieved by pressing a few buttons on the device. Here is a step-by-step guide for taking screenshots on the iPhone 6s:

First of all, press the Home button and the Sleep/Wake button at the same time. Doing this will save a full-screen screenshot of whatever you’re currently viewing on your iPhone 6s. The screenshot will be automatically saved in the Photos application.

If you want to take a screenshot of a specific portion of the screen, use the AssistiveTouch function. To enable AssistiveTouch, go to the Settings menu and select General, then Accessibility, and turn on AssistiveTouch. Once enabled, a floating menu will appear on the screen with options such as Home, Siri, and Custom. Tap on Custom and select Screenshot from the list.

To take the screenshot, simply drag your finger from the top left corner of the area you want to capture, to the bottom right corner. To save the screenshot, tap on the floating AssistiveTouch menu and select Save.

Finally, if you wish to share the screenshot you have taken, simply go to the Photos application and tap on the screenshot. You will be presented with the option to share the image. You can then choose where to share it such as with an email contact, on social media, or save the image to the cloud.
