1. Understanding the Feature#

Taking screenshots in Steam games is a great way to capture your gaming memories. However, players who have shared their screenshots with the Steam Community should be aware that anyone can re-share your screenshot again and again without your permission. If you want to get rid of this possibility, here is how to disable the feature to re-share the screenshot on Steam.

Firstly, launch Steam on your desktop. Find the View menu located at the top and click on Settings from the drop-down list. Then, select the In-Game tab and make sure that the Enable the Steam Overlay while in-game option is checked. Click on “OK” when you are done.

Next, open My Profile from the Steam library. Scroll down to the Screenshots tab. Select all the screenshots that you want to restrict from re-sharing by clicking on them. Under the “Public” section, uncheck the Allow anyone to view my screenshots in the Steam Community box.

Once you’ve finished, click on the Save button. Now, all the selected screenshots can only be viewed by users who have access to your profile. If someone tries to re-share any of them, it will appear broken as a result of the restriction.

In conclusion, by following these steps, you can easily disable the feature to re-share the screenshot in Steam. This will help you protect your screenshots from being shared without your permission.

The next step is to go to the ‘Privacy Settings’ section in your Steam account. In here, you can choose to delete any shared screenshots from your Steam account. Once you’ve done that, all shared screenshots will be deleted, ensuring that no one else can see them, no matter how they try.

Finally, if someone does manage to get a screenshot of your Steam account, you can flag it as inappropriate. In the ‘Account Settings’ section, you can find the ‘Flag Screenshots’ button. Once you’ve clicked on this, Steam will review the screenshot before taking action.

By following these steps, you can make sure that you have complete control over who sees your Steam screenshots.
