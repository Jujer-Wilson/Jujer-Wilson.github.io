i. Creating an iCloud Backup#

Backing up your contacts on an iPhone 6 is an important step to take when transferring to a new Android phone. It will ensure all of your contacts are transferred without any data loss. Fortunately, there are various methods for backing up contacts from your iPhone 6 and transferring them to your new Android device.

The easiest way to transfer contacts from an iPhone 6 to an Android device is using the Google Sync feature. By enabling Google Sync on your iPhone, all contacts will be backed up to Google and can then be automatically synced to your Android device. To do this, open your phone’s Settings app and then tap ‘Mail, Contacts, Calendars’ followed by ‘Add Account’. Then select ‘Google’ and sign in to your Google account. Once signed in, there should be an option to enable contacts sync.

Another common method of transferring contacts from an iPhone 6 to an Android device is using a cloud storage service. The iCloud service is pre-installed on all iPhones and can be used for backing up and transferring contacts. To do this, open the iCloud app and select ‘Contacts’. Then tap ‘Settings’ and select ‘Export vCard’. This will download all contacts as a .vcf file, which can then be imported into the Contacts app on your Android device.

You can back up your contacts manually as well. This is done by connecting your iPhone 6 to a PC or Mac, opening iTunes and then selecting ‘Back Up Now’. All contacts will be stored in an .ipd file which can then be imported into other devices.

Backing up your contacts from an iPhone 6 to an Android device is an important step in switching phones. By using Google Sync, iCloud, or manually exporting your contacts, you can easily ensure that all of your contacts are transferred over to your new device without any data loss.
