1. Preparing the Contacts on your iPhone 7#

There are many ways to transfer your contacts from an iPhone 7 to an Android device. This guide will take you through the process step-by-step. First, open the Contacts app on your iPhone 7 and tap the menu icon in the top left corner to open the settings. Select the option ‘Export Contacts’ and choose either vCard or iCloud depending on your preference. This will save your contacts to either your iCloud or your device storage. You can then transfer these contacts to your Android device using any of the methods explained in this guide.

I. Transferring Contacts from iPhone 7 to Android#

If you own an iPhone 7 and are interested in transferring your contacts to an Android phone, you’ll be pleased to know that the process is quite straightforward. All you need to get started is your iPhone and an Android device. First, make sure both the devices are switched on. Then, you’ll need to install a third-party app on the Android device, such as PhoneCopy, which is available on the Google Play Store. Once the app is installed, launch it and enter your credentials for the app. Now, go to your iPhone 7. Open the Settings app and go to Contacts. Tap the “Import/Export” option and select “Export to USB Storage.” Select the contacts you want to export and tap “Done”. Lastly, connect your Android device to the iPhone via USB. Your contacts will be transferred to the app on the Android device.

II. Syncing Contacts with iCloud#

Transferring your contacts from your iPhone 7 to an Android phone can be a simple process. All you need to do is export your contacts to a vCard-formatted .vcf file. Here are the steps involved:

First, go to “Settings” on your iPhone 7. Next, select “Contacts” and tap “Export Contacts”. Choose a filename for the vCard-formatted .vcf file and tap “Export”. After the file is exported, you can send the .vcf file as an attachment in an email or use Dropbox to upload the file.

Once the file is uploaded, on the Android phone, open the email or Dropbox app and download the file. Then, open the Contacts app, tap the menu button and select “Import/Export”. Choose the .vcf file you want to import and your contacts will be transferred from your iPhone 7 to the Android phone.

That’s all you need to do to transfer contacts from your iPhone 7 to an Android phone. Keep in mind, you might need to repeat these steps if you have multiple .vcf files. Now you are ready to use your Android phone with the contacts from your iPhone 7.

2. Transferring the Contacts through Google Contacts#

Transferring your contacts from iPhone 7 to your new Android device can be difficult if you do not know the steps. Fortunately, the process can be done quickly and easily with a few simple steps. First, make sure that you have your Google Contacts associated with your Google Account. To do this, open the Settings app on your iPhone and select ‘Passwords & Accounts.’ Here, tap on the ‘Add Account’ option and select ‘Google.’ After signing into your Google Account, turn on the slider for Contacts and ensure that the toggle is set to ‘On.’

Next, open the Google Contacts app on your iPhone and tap on the hamburger menu (three horizontal lines in the top left corner). Here, you will have the option to ‘Transfer’ contacts. Select the account that you want to transfer contacts from and choose ‘Transfer.’ Your contacts will then be transferred to your Google Account.

Finally, on your Android device, open the Settings app and select ‘Accounts.’ Here, select ‘Add Account’ and sign in to your Google Account. Once you have signed in, turn on the Contacts toggle to sync the contacts to your Android device. You can also access your contacts in the Google Contacts app.

By following the steps above, you can easily transfer your contacts from your iPhone 7 to your Android device. Doing so will keep all of your contacts in one place and ensure that they are safe and secure.

1. Downloading and Setting up Google Contacts#

Transferring contacts from iPhone 7 to Android phone is easy. The first step is to sync your iPhone contacts with Google Contacts. To do this, open the “Settings” app on your iPhone 7 and select “Contacts”. Tap “Add Account” and select “Google”. Then, enter your Google credentials and tap “Next”. Make sure the “Contacts” toggle is enabled and hit “Save”. All your contacts will be synced to your Google Contacts. Now, open the “Settings” app on your Android phone and select “Accounts”. Tap “Add Account” and select “Google”. Enter your Google credentials once again and tap “Next”. Make sure the “Contacts” toggle is enabled and hit “Save”. All your contacts from Google Contacts will be synced with your Android device. You have now successfully transferred your contacts from iPhone 7 to your Android phone.

2. Transferring Contacts to Android Device#

Transferring contacts from iPhone 7 to Android is an easy and straightforward process. First, sign into your Google account on your iPhone 7. After signing in, launch the Google Contacts app. Next, tap the menu icon located in the top-left corner of the screen. In the menu, choose the option Import. Then, select the contacts you want to transfer from your iPhone to your Google Contacts account. Finally, from the menu on the Android device, go to My Contacts and tap the More button to select the import option. Select the Google account you used to import the contacts from the iPhone 7 and all your contacts will be safely transferred over.

3. Verifying the Transfer and Completing the Process#

Once your phone backup is all set, it is time to verify and complete the process of transferring your contacts from iPhone 7 to Android. First, you can log in to your Google account from your Android device. Next, go to the Settings app, tap Accounts, and then tap Add account to add your Google account. After that, your contacts will automatically be synced and transferred to your Android device. Lastly, restart your device, and your contacts should be visible in the Contacts app. And you’re done! Congratulations, you have successfully transferred your contacts from iPhone 7 to Android.

1. Confirming the Contact Transfer#

After transferring contacts from your iPhone 7 to an Android phone, verification of the transfer is the next step. To do this, you should first open the Contacts app on your Android phone. Check that the contacts from your iPhone 7 are visible in the app. Once verified, the next step is to save the contacts to your device’s internal storage. This can be done by tapping on the ‘Save Contacts’ button in the Contacts app. Finally, click on the ‘Done’ button to complete the process. By completing these steps, you can easily transfer contacts from your iPhone 7 to an Android phone.

2. Finishing the Process#

Verifying the transfer and completing the process is an important step in transferring contacts from iPhone 7 to android phone. The process can be done by checking if the contacts have been successfully imported into your Android phone. First, open the contacts app on your Android phone and look for the contacts you wish to transfer. If the contacts are present, it means that the transfer process was successful. Secondly, delete the contacts from the iPhone 7 and this will complete the process. Lastly, you can also check if the contacts have been transferred to the cloud storage of your Android phone. This will provide an additional layer of security and ensure that the contacts will be safe even if the phone is lost.
