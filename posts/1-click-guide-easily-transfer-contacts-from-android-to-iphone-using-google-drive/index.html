First off, you need to set up your Google account on your Android device. Then open the Contacts app and select “Manage Contacts.” From there, tap More followed by Export to begin the exporting process. Select Google as the format of the contacts and log in to your Google account to finalize the export. With the contacts now saved in your Google Drive, you can now move to your iPhone. Begin by opening the Settings app and select Mail, Contacts, Calendars. From there, tap Add Account and select Google to log in with your Google account. With your Google account linked to your iPhone, head to the Contacts app to begin the import process. From the top left corner, tap Groups and select All Google Contacts. 1. Connecting Your Android Device to Google Drive#

Transferring contacts from Android to iPhone can be difficult. However, if you have access to a Google Drive account, you can transfer your contacts with ease. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to do it. Firstly, on your Android device, go to Contacts and select the More option. From the list of options, select Export and then choose Google CSV. Secondly, go to your Google Drive account, and select New > File Upload. Upload the CSV file you just exported from your Android device. Finally, open your iPhone and go to Settings > Mail, Contacts, Calendars > Import Contacts. Select the Google CSV file you just uploaded to Google Drive, and your contacts will be imported to your iPhone. With this simple process, you can easily transfer your contacts from your Android to your iPhone using Google Drive.

2. Transferring Contacts to iPhone from Google Drive#

One of the best things about Google Drive is that it makes it easy to transfer contacts from Android to iPhone devices. To do this, all you need is a few minutes of your time and an active Google account. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to transfer contacts from Android to iPhone using Google Drive.

First, open the “Settings” app on your Android device. Go to the “Accounts and backup” section and tap “Accounts”. Choose the Google account you’d like to use and tap “Sync Contacts”. This will enable contact sync with your Google account.

Next, open the Google Drive app on your iPhone device. Log in with the same Google account and tap the “Menu” icon in the top left corner of the screen. Select “Backup” and tap “Restore”. From the list of available devices, select the one that contains your Google contacts.

Your contacts will then be transferred to your iPhone. To make sure the transfer was successful, go to the “Contacts” app and check if your contacts are all there. That’s it! You have now successfully transferred your contacts from Android to iPhone using Google Drive.
